WAIT, I QUALIFY?!  —  EPISODE 2:  "Getting Connected"

SHAWNA:  Welcome back to "Wait, I Qualify?!" -- the show about unlocking aid for real people, right here in Nevada County. I'm Shawna.

JEFF:  And I'm Jeff. We're from TechEmpower dot org -- a local nonprofit that helps folks find the help and incentives they're entitled to, from the state, the federal government, and local organizations.

SHAWNA:  Today we're talking about getting connected -- internet and phone service. Help has moved online. Job applications. Doctor visits. School. Even applying for help itself.

JEFF:  Right -- you practically need the internet to get help affording the internet. So today we cut through that. And we didn't just bring numbers this time -- -- we brought the stuff. A phone plan for zero dollars a month. Home internet for about fifteen. A little box from the library that IS the internet -- we'll get there. And a real computer, with a warranty, for about a hundred and fifty bucks. All real, all available right here, right now.

SHAWNA:  Okay -- start with the phone.

JEFF:  The phone is the best connection deal in California, full stop. Two programs stack on top of each other. The federal one is called Lifeline -- about nine dollars a month off your phone or internet bill. Then California adds its own, called California LifeLine -- up to nineteen dollars more. Stack them, and that's almost thirty dollars a month in help. Which is why companies like Assurance Wireless and SafeLink Wireless can hand you a smartphone -- like this one, the phone itself included -- with unlimited talk, unlimited text, and a pile of data, for ZERO dollars a month.

SHAWNA:  Free free? Or "free, asterisk, gotcha on page nine" free?

JEFF:  Free free. No bill, no contract, no credit check. And the income lines are higher than people guess -- this is "Wait, I Qualify?!", after all. A single person can make up to about twenty-four thousand six hundred a year. A family of four, up to fifty thousand eight hundred. And if anyone in your house is already on CalFresh -- that's the grocery money program -- or Medi-Cal, the health coverage, or W-I-C, or your kid gets a free school lunch... you qualify automatically. No income math needed.

SHAWNA:  So one "yes" anywhere on that list, and the phone is yours.

JEFF:  Exactly. And signing up is the easy part: you do it right on the phone company's own website -- Assurance Wireless or SafeLink Wireless -- one short form, and the company manages the whole state application for you from there. Want to see every company first? californialifeline dot com lists all the providers for your zip code.

SHAWNA:  Fine print we should be straight about?

JEFF:  Three things. It's one benefit per household -- one, total, phone or internet, not both. You have to actually use the free plan at least once a month, or they shut it off. And once a year they re-check that you still qualify -- don't ignore that letter. Oh -- and ask which network the free phone rides on. Here's the thing most people don't know: there are really only THREE cell networks in the whole country -- Verizon, A-T-and-T, and T-Mobile -- and every one of these free phones runs on one of the three. Most of these companies use T-Mobile, which is solid here in town but spotty in the canyons. So we did the homework for you: on our LifeLine resource page at techempower dot org, we list which of the three networks each company uses -- so you can pick one that actually reaches your road. If you're keeping a landline instead, these discounts work on a home phone too.

SHAWNA:  Okay, phones -- handled. What about internet at the house?

JEFF:  In this county, step one is always "check your address." No program matters if the wire doesn't reach your road. But here's what's real. If you're in Grass Valley or Nevada City proper, the cable company's program is called Xfinity Internet Essentials: fourteen ninety-five a month, seventy-five megabits -- that's plenty for streaming and video calls -- free equipment, no contract. One catch: it's for households new to Xfinity. Same easy eligibility: CalFresh, Medi-Cal, school lunch, W-I-C, and more. And the sign-up is genuinely short -- here's the actual screen. And if AT&T reaches your address instead of the cable company, they've got their own low-income plan -- AT&T Access. Thirty dollars a month or less, a free modem, and the same easy eligibility as the rest: SNAP, S-S-I, or a veteran's pension. You can even stack that federal Lifeline discount on top. It's at att dot com slash access.

SHAWNA:  And Truckee?

JEFF:  Different cable company over the hill -- Optimum -- so Truckee folks, check that one instead. Now -- my favorite one. There is a tiny LOCAL internet company east of Nevada City called NevCoFiber, and they have a fifteen-dollar-a- month plan for qualifying households. And that's not slow internet -- that's FIBER. No data caps. And their network has backup power, so it stays up when P-G-and-E shuts the power off -- though you'll still need a little power at your house to run your own router.

SHAWNA:  Fifteen dollars... for fiber... that survives a power shutoff. Where's the catch?

JEFF:  The catch is reach -- they're over a hundred homes around Banner Mountain and Red Dog Road so far, with a couple hundred more being built right now. If you're anywhere east of Nevada City, check nevcofiber dot com -- there's a spot right on the front page to check your address.

SHAWNA:  And if none of them reach you today?

JEFF:  Three real answers. First: if the wire doesn't reach your house, the state has a program that helps pay to EXTEND it -- it's called the Line Extension Program. Same income lines as the phone discount -- or if you're on CARE, the utility discount, you're in. And the practical part: the internet company can file the application for you. So when a provider says "we don't serve your road," your next question is: "will you check the Line Extension Program for me?" Second: Nevada County landed one of the biggest rural broadband allocations in California -- almost seventy-five million dollars set aside by the state -- and so far nearly fifteen million of it has turned into two local fiber projects, with crews already building. Third: since this January, that LifeLine discount we talked about also works on home internet -- up to twenty or thirty dollars a month off. Which providers offer it depends on your address -- look them up at californialifeline dot com. So if the answer is "no" today, check again in a few months. It's changing fast. And plenty of foothill homes skip the wire entirely -- fixed wireless, from local companies like Smarter Broadband or Treelink, or satellite like Starlink. Those aren't the low-income discount programs, but out here they're often what actually reaches you.

SHAWNA:  And meanwhile?

JEFF:  Meanwhile... there's a place in this county handing out free internet, today, no monthly bill at all. The library.

SHAWNA:  Okay -- what does the library have?

JEFF:  Your Nevada County library card can check out THE INTERNET. It's a little box called a hotspot -- this one, right here. We checked it out from the library this week, the same way you'd borrow a book. Free. Fourteen days. UNLIMITED data. It makes WiFi for up to ten devices -- your phone, the kids' homework laptop, all of it. And here's the genius part for our county: the library stocks them on Verizon, T-Mobile, AND A-T-and-T -- so you pick the one that actually reaches your road.

SHAWNA:  Unlimited data, free, for two weeks. Who can check one out?

JEFF:  Any adult or teen cardholder in good standing -- and teens, bring a parent or guardian, because they sign the form with you. It's a quick form at the desk. If they're checked out, put a hold on one in the catalog, same as a bestseller -- it looks like this. Two honest notes: fourteen days means fourteen days -- the box shuts itself off if you keep it late. And the card shows a two-hundred-dollar charge if you lose it -- bring it back, charge disappears. Which is why THIS one goes back on Tuesday.

SHAWNA:  And what else comes with the card?

JEFF:  Every branch has free WiFi that works from the PARKING LOT, twenty-four hours a day -- the Nevada City lot is a free internet cafe at midnight, no card needed. Free computers inside. Chromebooks you can use in the building. Penn Valley and Bear River even have after-hours access for adults -- sign up once, do a quick orientation, and after that your card unlocks the door when staff have gone home. And -- this one matters -- free one-on-one tech help. A real person will sit down with you and your phone or laptop -- by appointment at the Madelyn Helling and Truckee branches, plus drop-in help. No judgment, no charge.

SHAWNA:  One caution from our homework: branch hours just changed at the start of July -- so check the hours before you drive over. The WiFi in the parking lot, though -- always on.

SHAWNA:  And that library hotspot isn't the only free one.

JEFF:  Not for families with school-age kids. T-Mobile runs a program called Project Ten Million. If you've got a child in kindergarten through twelfth grade, your household can get its OWN hotspot -- free -- with free internet on it: two hundred gigabytes a year, for up to five years. Same doorway as everything else -- if your kid gets free school lunch, you almost certainly qualify. You apply right to T-Mobile, and it ships to your door.

SHAWNA:  So the library lends you one -- and if there's a student in the house, T-Mobile gives you one of your own.

SHAWNA:  That leaves the computer itself. Because a new laptop is five hundred dollars before tax, and that's just not happening for a lot of households.

JEFF:  So don't buy new. There's a nonprofit up in Chico called Computers for Classrooms that refurbishes business computers for California families. About a hundred and fifty dollars gets you a desktop -- WITH the monitor and keyboard -- Windows and Office installed, and a ONE-YEAR warranty. They ship it right to your door. Laptops too, around the same price. If money's tight, that is the best computer deal in the state -- and ordering is just a web store.

SHAWNA:  A warranty. On a hundred-fifty-dollar computer.

JEFF:  A real warranty -- they're a nonprofit, the mission IS the warranty. Another one, called human-I-T, sells refurbished Chromebooks -- like this one -- for around ninety to a hundred thirty dollars. And if you've got cell signal at home, they offer unlimited hotspot internet for about fifteen dollars a month -- you just buy the little hotspot box first, about seventy-five dollars, one time.

SHAWNA:  Okay -- and for the folks who get all this gear home, set it on the kitchen table, and feel completely lost?

JEFF:  Free humans. Three flavors. If you're sixty or older: Senior Planet, from A-A-R-P -- free classes over video, and a free HOTLINE where patient trainers answer any tech question -- the number will be on your screen. If your ears, eyes, or hands make a regular phone hard to use: California Connect ships you a free adapted phone -- amplified, big-button, captioned -- no income test at all; you just need a doctor or other licensed professional to sign off that you need it. And right here at home: that's us -- TechEmpower. Free help from real neighbors, online, whenever you're stuck: go to techempower dot org and click "Join our Discord." Get that hundred-fifty-dollar computer home and we'll walk you through setting it up, step by step. That's literally why we exist.

SHAWNA:  Let's land the plane. Today we found: a smartphone plan for zero dollars a month. Home internet for about fifteen. A library card that lends you unlimited internet -- this little box -- and a parking lot that's online at midnight. A warrantied computer for about a hundred and fifty. And free, patient humans to help you use all of it.

JEFF:  And if you have no internet at all tonight, every door into help still opens by phone. Dial two-one-one -- here that's two-one-one Connecting Point -- free, confidential, around the clock, English and Spanish. If you're struggling emotionally, call or text nine-eight-eight, any phone, no internet needed. Online, it's findhelp dot org -- and us, at techempower dot org, where the Discord button gets you one-on-one help from real neighbors.
