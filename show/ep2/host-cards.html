Jeff · J1Phone numbers (LifeLine stack)
  • PROP: phone up at the ZERO-dollars reveal → hand it to Shawna (“like this one” — it’s a stand-in)
  • Federal Lifeline ≈ $9/mo + California LifeLine up to $19/mo = almost $30/mo → $0 smartphone plans, phone included (Assurance Wireless, SafeLink Wireless)
  • Income lines: $24,600 single / $50,800 family of 4 — OR auto-qualify via CalFresh, Medi-Cal, WIC, school lunch (ad-lib care: WIC/lunch = the state side; “the company sorts out which discounts you get”)
  • Apply on the carrier’s OWN website (assurancewireless.com / safelinkwireless.com) — one short form, they manage the state application · full provider list: californialifeline.com
  • Gotchas: one per household · use it monthly or it shuts off · annual re-check · mostly T-Mobile network (spotty in canyons) · works on a landline too
Jeff · J2Internet numbers
  • Xfinity Internet Essentials: $14.95/mo, 75 Mbps, free equipment, no contract — GV + Nevada City cores; Truckee = Optimum, not Comcast
  • AT&T Access: $30/mo or less, free modem — where AT&T reaches the address; SNAP / SSI / Veterans-pension eligibility; can stack federal Lifeline on top; att.com/access
  • NevCoFiber Community Assist: $15/mo fiber, no caps, battery-backed through PG&E shutoffs — Banner Mtn / Red Dog area, expanding; nevcofiber.com
  • Line Extension Program (CASF/CPUC): state grant pays to EXTEND the wire to unserved homes — LifeLine/CARE income lines (or equivalent income) — rolling applications — the provider can file it for you (“will you check the Line Extension Program for me?”)
  • Hope: ~$75M state money earmarked for unserved Nevada County homes; LifeLine home-internet discount ($20–30/mo) rolling out this year — re-check your address every few months
