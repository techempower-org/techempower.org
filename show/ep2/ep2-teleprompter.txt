BENEFIT ONE: THE ZERO-DOLLAR PHONE PLAN
================================================================

SHAWNA:
Okay -- start with the phone.

[PROP: JEFF picks up the smartphone, holds it at chest
height, screen toward camera]

JEFF:
The phone is the best connection deal in California, full
stop. Two programs stack on top of each other. The federal
one is called Lifeline -- about nine dollars a month off
your phone or internet bill. Then California adds its own,
called California LifeLine -- up to nineteen dollars more.
Stack them, and that's almost thirty dollars a month in
help. Which is why companies like Assurance Wireless and
SafeLink Wireless can hand you a smartphone -- like this one, the
phone itself included -- with unlimited talk, unlimited
text, and a pile of data, for ZERO dollars a month.

[PROP: JEFF hands the phone to SHAWNA]

SHAWNA:
[turning the phone over, inspecting it]
Free free? Or "free, asterisk, gotcha on page nine" free?

JEFF:
Free free. No bill, no contract, no credit check. And the
income lines are higher than people guess -- this is "Wait,
I Qualify?!", after all. A single person can make up to
about twenty-four thousand six hundred a year. A family of
four, up to fifty thousand eight hundred. And if anyone in
your house is already on CalFresh -- that's the grocery
money program -- or Medi-Cal, the health coverage, or W-I-C,
or your kid gets a free school lunch... you qualify
automatically. No income math needed.

SHAWNA:
[setting the phone down on the desk, face up]
So one "yes" anywhere on that list, and the phone is yours.

JEFF:
Exactly. And signing up is the easy part: you do it right on
the phone company's own website -- Assurance Wireless or
SafeLink Wireless -- one short form, and the company manages
the whole state application for you from there. Want to see
every company first? californialifeline dot com lists all
the providers for your zip code.

[POST: SCREENCAST -- assurancewireless.com sign-up start:
the apply flow's first screen. ~6 seconds, rides over the
dialogue above]

[POST: SCREENCAST -- californialifeline.com provider list
for a Nevada County zip. ~4 seconds]

[POST: PROGRAM CARD -- California LifeLine free phone plans:
apply at assurancewireless.com / safelinkwireless.com --
full provider list: californialifeline.com]

SHAWNA:
Fine print we should be straight about?

JEFF:
Three things. It's one benefit per household -- one, total,
phone or internet, not both. You have to actually use the
free plan at least once a month, or they shut it off. And
once a year they re-check that you still qualify -- don't
ignore that letter. Oh -- and ask which network the free
phone rides on. Most of them use T-Mobile, which is solid
here in town and spotty in the canyons. If you're keeping a
landline instead, these discounts work on a home phone too.

BENEFIT TWO: HOME INTERNET FOR ABOUT FIFTEEN DOLLARS
================================================================

SHAWNA:
Okay, phones -- handled. What about internet at the house?

JEFF:
In this county, step one is always
"check your address." No program matters if the wire doesn't
reach your road. But here's what's real. If you're in Grass
Valley or Nevada City proper, the cable company's program is
called Xfinity Internet Essentials: fourteen ninety-five a
month, seventy-five megabits -- that's plenty for streaming
and video calls -- free equipment, no contract. One
catch: it's for households new to Xfinity. Same easy
eligibility: CalFresh, Medi-Cal, school lunch, W-I-C, and
more. And the sign-up is genuinely short -- here's the
actual screen.

[POST: SCREENCAST -- Xfinity Internet Essentials page:
eligibility checklist + the $14.95 plan tile + Check
Availability address box. ~7 seconds, rides over dialogue]

SHAWNA:
And Truckee?

JEFF:
Different cable company over the hill -- Optimum -- so
Truckee folks, check that one instead. Now -- my favorite
one. There is a tiny LOCAL internet company east of Nevada
City called NevCoFiber, and they have a fifteen-dollar-a-
month plan for qualifying households. And that's not slow
internet -- that's FIBER. No data caps. And their network
has backup power, so it stays up when P-G-and-E shuts the
power off -- though you'll still need a little power at your
house to run your own router.

SHAWNA:
Fifteen dollars... for fiber... that survives a power
shutoff. Where's the catch?

JEFF:
The catch is reach -- they're over a hundred homes around
Banner Mountain and Red Dog Road so far, with a couple
hundred more being built right now. If you're anywhere east
of Nevada City, check nevcofiber dot com -- there's a spot
right on the front page to check your address.

[POST: SCREENCAST -- nevcofiber.com address/coverage check:
type an address, availability answer appears. ~6 seconds]

[POST: PROGRAM CARD -- Xfinity Internet Essentials $14.95 /
NevCoFiber Community Assist $15 -- "check your address"]

SHAWNA:
And if none of them reach you today?

JEFF:
Three real answers. First: if the wire doesn't reach your
house, the state has a program that helps pay to EXTEND it
-- it's called the Line Extension Program. Same income lines
as the phone discount -- or if you're on CARE, the utility
discount, you're in. And the practical part: the internet
company can file the application for you. So when a provider
says "we don't serve your road," your next question is:
"will you check the Line Extension Program for me?"

[POST: PROGRAM CARD -- CASF Line Extension Program: state
grant to extend the line to unserved homes -- LifeLine/CARE
income lines -- ask your provider to apply for you]

JEFF (cont):
Second: Nevada County landed one of the biggest rural
broadband allocations in California -- almost seventy-five
million dollars set aside by the state -- and so far nearly
fifteen million of it has turned into two local fiber
projects, with crews already building. Third: since this
January, that LifeLine discount we talked about also works
on home internet -- up to twenty or thirty dollars a month
off -- through participating providers like Xfinity and
Spectrum, with more signing on. So if the answer is "no"
today, check again in a few months. It's changing fast.

SHAWNA:
And meanwhile?

JEFF:
Meanwhile... there's a place in this county handing out free
internet, today, no monthly bill at all. The library.
