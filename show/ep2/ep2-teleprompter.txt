================================================================
THE MYTH: "THE FREE INTERNET PROGRAM IS GONE, SO THERE'S NOTHING"
================================================================

SHAWNA:
Before we get to those -- Jeff, I have to ask, because half
our viewers are thinking it. There WAS a big internet
discount from the government. Thirty dollars a month. My
neighbor had it. And then it just... vanished?

JEFF:
Your neighbor remembers right. It was called the Affordable
Connectivity Program -- the big pandemic-era internet
discount. At its peak it was helping over twenty-three
million households. And in June of twenty twenty-four,
Congress let the money run out. It ended. And as of today,
nothing federal has replaced it.

SHAWNA:
So if something online promises "free government internet"
through that program today...

JEFF:
Walk away -- that's a scam. The program is gone. But here's
the part nobody told those twenty-three million people: the
help that SURVIVED is better than most folks realize. And
that's the rest of today's show.


================================================================
BENEFIT ONE: THE ZERO-DOLLAR PHONE PLAN
================================================================

SHAWNA:
Okay -- start with the phone.

JEFF:
The phone is the best connection deal in California, full
stop. Two programs stack on top of each other. The federal
one is called Lifeline -- about nine dollars a month off
your phone or internet bill. Then California adds its own,
called California LifeLine -- up to nineteen dollars more.
Stack them, and that's almost thirty dollars a month in
help. Which is why companies like Assurance Wireless and
TruConnect can hand you a smartphone -- the phone itself
included -- with unlimited talk, unlimited text, and a pile
of data, for ZERO dollars a month.

SHAWNA:
Free free? Or "free, asterisk, gotcha on page nine" free?

JEFF:
Free free. No bill, no contract, no credit check. And the
income lines are higher than people guess -- this is "Wait,
I Qualify?!", after all. A single person can make up to
about twenty-four thousand six hundred a year. A family of
four, up to fifty thousand eight hundred. And if anyone in
your house is already on CalFresh -- that's the grocery
money program -- or Medi-Cal, the health coverage, or W-I-C,
or your kid gets a free school lunch... you qualify
automatically. No income math needed.

SHAWNA:
So basically -- if you applied for anything from episode
one, this one's yours too.

JEFF:
Exactly. One stop: californialifeline dot com -- there's a
provider search, type in your zip code, pick a company, and
they walk you through it. One heads-up: as of this February,
the state program and the federal program each want their
own application -- it's two forms now, not one. Annoying,
but the phone company helps you do both.

[POST: PROGRAM CARD -- California LifeLine: californialifeline.com,
free plans via Assurance Wireless / TruConnect]

SHAWNA:
Fine print we should be straight about?

JEFF:
Three things. It's one benefit per household -- one, total,
phone or internet, not both. You have to actually use the
free plan at least once a month, or they shut it off. And
once a year they re-check that you still qualify -- don't
ignore that letter. Oh -- and ask which network the free
phone rides on. Most of them use T-Mobile, which is solid
here in town and spotty in the canyons. If you're keeping a
landline instead, these discounts work on a home phone too.


================================================================
BENEFIT TWO: HOME INTERNET FOR ABOUT FIFTEEN DOLLARS
================================================================

SHAWNA:
Okay, phones -- handled. What about internet at the house?
And give us the honest version, because half this county
lives past the end of the cable line.

JEFF:
The honest version: in this county, step one is always
"check your address." No program matters if the wire doesn't
reach your road. But here's what's real. If you're in Grass
Valley or Nevada City proper, the cable company's program is
called Xfinity Internet Essentials: fourteen ninety-five a
month, seventy-five megabits -- that's plenty for streaming
and video calls -- free equipment, no contract. One
catch: it's for households new to Xfinity. Same easy
eligibility: CalFresh, Medi-Cal, school lunch, W-I-C, and
more.

SHAWNA:
And Truckee?

JEFF:
Different cable company over the hill -- Optimum -- so
Truckee folks, check that one instead. Now -- my favorite
one. There is a tiny LOCAL internet company east of Nevada
City called NevCoFiber, and they have a fifteen-dollar-a-
month plan for qualifying households. And that's not slow
internet -- that's FIBER. No data caps. And their network
has backup power, so it stays up when P-G-and-E shuts the
power off -- though you'll still need a little power at your
house to run your own router.

SHAWNA:
Fifteen dollars... for fiber... that survives a power
shutoff. Where's the catch?

JEFF:
The catch is reach -- they're over a hundred homes around
Banner Mountain and Red Dog Road so far, with a couple
hundred more being built right now. If you're anywhere east
of Nevada City, check nevcofiber dot com.

[POST: PROGRAM CARD -- Xfinity Internet Essentials $14.95 /
NevCoFiber Community Assist $15 -- "check your address"]

SHAWNA:
And if none of them reach you today?

JEFF:
Two real reasons for hope. First: Nevada County landed one
of the biggest rural broadband allocations in California --
almost seventy-five million dollars set aside by the state
-- and so far nearly fifteen million of it has turned into
two local fiber projects, with crews already building.
Second: since this January, that LifeLine discount we talked
about also works on home internet -- up to twenty or thirty
dollars a month off -- through participating providers like
Xfinity and Spectrum, with more signing on. So if the answer
is "no" today, check again in a few months. It's changing
fast.

SHAWNA:
And meanwhile?

JEFF:
Meanwhile... there's a place in this county handing out free
internet, today, no monthly bill at all. The library.


================================================================
BENEFIT THREE: THE LIBRARY CHECKS OUT THE INTERNET
================================================================

SHAWNA:
Okay, you've been sitting on this one all week -- go.

JEFF:
Your Nevada County library card can check out THE INTERNET.
It's a little box called a hotspot -- you borrow it like a
book. Free. Fourteen days. UNLIMITED data. It makes WiFi for
up to ten devices -- your phone, the kids' homework laptop,
all of it. And here's the genius part for our county: the
library stocks them on Verizon, T-Mobile, AND A-T-and-T --
so you pick the one that actually reaches your road.

SHAWNA:
Hold on. The library will hand me unlimited internet, for
free, for two weeks. With a straight face.

JEFF:
With a smile, actually. Any adult or teen cardholder in good
standing -- and teens, bring a parent or guardian, because
they sign the form with you. It's a quick form at the desk.
If they're checked out, put a hold on one in the catalog,
same as a bestseller. Two honest notes: fourteen days means
fourteen days -- the box shuts itself off if you keep it
late. And the card shows a two-hundred-dollar charge if you
lose it -- bring it back, charge disappears.

SHAWNA:
And the library card itself costs...

JEFF:
Nothing. And the branches keep giving: every branch has free
WiFi that works from the PARKING LOT, twenty-four hours a
day -- the Grass Valley lot is a free internet cafe at
midnight, no card needed. Free computers inside. Chromebooks
you can use in the building. Penn Valley and Bear River even
have after-hours access for adults -- sign up once, do a
quick orientation, and after that your card unlocks the door
when staff have gone home. And -- this one matters -- free
one-on-one tech help. A real person will sit down with you
and your phone or laptop -- by appointment at the Madelyn
Helling and Truckee branches, plus drop-in help. No
judgment, no charge.

[POST: PROGRAM CARD -- Nevada County Library: free hotspot
lending, 24/7 WiFi, free tech help -- 530-265-7050]

SHAWNA:
One caution from our homework: branch hours just changed at
the start of July -- so check the hours before you drive
over. The WiFi in the parking lot, though -- always on.

BENEFIT FOUR: A REAL COMPUTER + FREE HUMANS TO HELP
================================================================

SHAWNA:
That leaves the computer itself. Because a new laptop is
five hundred dollars before tax, and that's just not
happening for a lot of households.

JEFF:
So don't buy new. There's a nonprofit up in Chico called
Computers for Classrooms that refurbishes business computers
for California families. About a hundred and fifty dollars
gets you a desktop -- WITH the monitor and keyboard --
Windows and Office installed, and a ONE-YEAR warranty. They
ship it right to your door. Laptops too, around the same
price. If money's tight, that is the best computer deal in
the state.

SHAWNA:
A warranty. On a hundred-fifty-dollar computer.

JEFF:
A real warranty -- they're a nonprofit, the mission IS the
warranty. Another one, called human-I-T, sells refurbished
Chromebooks for around ninety to a hundred thirty dollars --
and if you've got cell signal at home, they offer unlimited
hotspot internet for about fifteen dollars a month -- you
just buy the little hotspot box first, about seventy-five
dollars, one time.

[POST: PROGRAM CARD -- Computers for Classrooms (ships in CA) /
human-I-T -- numbers on screen]

SHAWNA:
Okay -- and for the folks who get all this gear home, set it
on the kitchen table, and feel completely lost?

JEFF:
Free humans. Three flavors. If you're sixty or older: Senior
Planet, from A-A-R-P -- free classes over video, and a free
HOTLINE where patient trainers answer any tech question --
the number will be on your screen. If your ears, eyes, or
hands make a regular phone hard to use: California Connect
ships you a free adapted phone -- amplified, big-button,
captioned -- no income test at all; you just need a doctor
or other licensed professional to sign off that you need
it. And right here at home:
that's us -- TechEmpower. Free help from real neighbors,
online, whenever you're stuck: go to techempower dot org and
click "Join our Discord." Get that hundred-fifty-dollar
computer home and we'll walk you through setting it up, step
by step. That's literally why we exist.
