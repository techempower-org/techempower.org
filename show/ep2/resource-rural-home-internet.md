# Getting Home Internet in Rural Nevada County

**Rule zero: check your address, not the map.** Every provider's coverage map over-promises out here — it can't see the ridge, the canyon, or the trees between you and a tower. The only truth is what actually reaches *your* address, so use each provider's "check availability" tool before you count on anything.

There are two tiers below: **the affordable/discount programs to try first**, and **the rural options that reach you when the wire doesn't** (regular price, but real).

## 2. Past the cable/fiber line? The rural options that actually reach you

These aren't low-income discounts — they're **regular-price**, but for much of the foothills they're how you get online at all.

- **Fixed wireless (line-of-sight to a tower).** The rural workhorse. Local providers:
  - **Smarter Broadband** — fixed wireless serving rural Western Nevada + Placer (based near Alta Sierra); up to ~100 Mbps, plans from roughly **$49/mo**. smarterbroadband.com
  - **Treelink** — a neighborhood mesh fixed-wireless service around Nevada City. treelink-style mesh (uses AT&T/Comcast as its "exit" to the internet).
  - **Cal.net** — fixed wireless in *parts* of the area; note its footprint is thin here (its own service list covers **Grass Valley but not Nevada City**), so check your exact address. Also a California LifeLine broadband provider where it reaches.
  - *All fixed wireless needs a clear line of sight to the provider's tower — availability is very address-specific.*
- **Satellite** (when nothing on the ground reaches you):
  - **Starlink** — low-orbit satellite, **low latency** (good for video calls / normal use); higher cost (equipment + ~monthly service — verify current pricing). The good satellite option.
  - **Viasat / HughesNet** — geostationary satellite, **high latency (~600 ms+)** — usable for email/browsing but frustrating for video calls or anything real-time. A genuine last resort when it's the *only* thing available.

## 3. If literally nothing reaches you yet

- **CASF Line Extension Program** (state, via the CPUC): a grant that helps pay to **extend the wire** to unserved homes. Same income lines as the phone LifeLine (or CARE). **Your internet provider can file the application for you** — so ask: *"Will you check the Line Extension Program for my address?"*
- **It's changing fast:** Nevada County has ~$75M in state broadband money earmarked for unserved homes, with local fiber projects underway. If the answer is "no" today, re-check in a few months.

## How to check + local maps
- **californialifeline.com** — the LifeLine phone/internet discount + the per-ZIP provider list.
- **nevadacountyca.gov** — the county's Local Broadband Providers & Coverage Maps page.
- **jphein.com/internet-options-in-nevada-county** — a local resident's plain-language field guide to the real options.
