--- SCRIPT ---

AT&T just got federal permission to end the old copper
landline for more than a hundred eighty thousand
California homes. Nothing changes before June of twenty
twenty-seven -- and no court has ordered it.

But here's the fight that affects all of us. California
has one rule that protects you: a phone company can't cut
off your line unless someone else can actually serve your
home. AT&T is asking to erase that rule.

Why it matters where we live: when the power's out for
days, or you're in a canyon with no signal, the old copper
phone still works -- it powers itself. The replacements
don't. AT&T's own paperwork admits that with no power, its
new service "will not work, including emergency
nine-one-one."

Here's your part. The California Public Utilities
Commission is deciding this now, and they want to hear
from you. Call their Public Advisor, toll-free --
eight-six-six, eight-four-nine, eight-three-nine-zero --
and tell them what your phone really does when the power
goes out.

Your voice counts. Techempower dot org -- join our
Discord, we'll walk you through it.
