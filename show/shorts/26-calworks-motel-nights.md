# Homeless and on CalWORKs? 16 Nights in a Motel.

- **Est. length:** ~55 s (~130 spoken words)
- **Source:** Ep3/Ep4 fact-check + corpus rule `calworks-homeless-assistance` (Temporary HA up to 16 nights; Permanent HA = security deposit + last month's rent; once per 12-month period; apply via form CW-42; open to CalWORKs recipients OR apparently-eligible applicants). New `/resources` row added 2026-07-05.
- **Freshness:** Confirm the 16-nights + once-per-12-months terms on the CDSS Homeless Assistance page before recording; Nevada County apply line 530-265-1340; re-check after fiscal-year updates.

--- SCRIPT ---

If your family gets CalWORKs -- or could qualify for it --
and you're homeless or about to lose your housing, there's
emergency help most people never hear about.

It's called CalWORKs Homeless Assistance. It can put your
family up in a motel for up to sixteen nights while you
look for a place. And when you find one, it can help pay a
security deposit and last month's rent, too.

You can use it once every twelve months. And here's the
part people miss -- you don't have to already be on
CalWORKs. If your family would qualify, you can apply for
this at the same time you apply for the rest.

Ask your county worker for -- quote -- the Homeless
Assistance program, form C-W forty-two. In Nevada County,
call five-three-oh, two-six-five, one-three-four-zero.
