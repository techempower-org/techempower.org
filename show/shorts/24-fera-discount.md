# Make a Little Too Much for CARE? There's FERA.

- **Est. length:** ~50 s (~120 spoken words)
- **Source:** Ep4 (Home + Transportation) fact-check — FERA (Family Electric Rate Assistance); corpus rule `fera` (250% FPG; ~18% off electric; ~$45/mo typical; HH4 up to ~$82,500/yr; SB1130 opened 1–2 person households; one application covers CARE + FERA).
- **Freshness:** Income table effective through May 31, 2027 — re-pull after. Confirm the ~18% electric figure + one-application-covers-both on pge.com/carefera before recording.

--- SCRIPT ---

If you've heard of PG&E's CARE discount but figured you
earn a little too much for it -- there's a second program
built for exactly you. It's called FERA.

FERA -- Family Electric Rate Assistance -- takes about
eighteen percent off the electric side of your PG&E bill.
For a typical home that's roughly forty-five dollars a
month, every month you qualify.

A family of four can earn up to about eighty-two thousand
a year and still get it. And here's the myth to bust: FERA
used to be closed to one- and two-person households. That
rule is gone -- singles and couples qualify now too.

The best part -- one application covers both CARE and FERA,
and PG&E just puts you on whichever fits. Apply at
pge.com slash carefera.
