- **Est. length:** ~57 seconds (≈140 spoken words)
- **Source:** Ep4 — car-bar.json claims `retirement-amounts`, `retirement-running-car`, `fiscal-year-funding` (verified 2026-07-01 vs live bar.ca.gov/cap/retirement): the $1,350 no-income-test tier REQUIRES a failed most-recent smog check; income-eligible tops out at $2,000 (tiers now stated explicitly: $2,000 requires failed smog; passed-smog max is $1,500 — corrected 2026-07-03 after the E5 live pass found the old "even for a car that PASSED…$2,000" phrasing overstated); car must be registered + driveable; registration lapse >120 days in the prior two years disqualifies; funds reset each July 1 and can run dry.

--- SCRIPT ---

That car that just failed smog? The state of
California will BUY it from you.

It's called vehicle retirement. If your car failed its
most recent smog check, the state pays thirteen
hundred fifty dollars to take it off the road -- no
income test at all. Income-qualified, a failed-smog car
can get up to two thousand -- and even a car that
PASSED can get up to fifteen hundred.

Two catches, so nobody wastes a trip. The car has to
be registered and driveable. And if the registration
sat lapsed more than about four months over the last
two years, it won't qualify.

The money comes from a state budget that resets every
July -- and it can run dry, so don't sit on this.

Apply at bar dot C-A dot gov, slash cap. Or dial
two-one-one -- or ask us at techempower dot org, on
our Discord.
