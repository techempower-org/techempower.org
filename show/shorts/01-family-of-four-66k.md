# A Family of Four Making Sixty-Six Thousand Can Still Qualify

- **Est. length:** ~55 seconds (≈150 spoken words)
- **Source:** Ep1 — claims `fpl-66k` (200% of 2026 FPL = $66,000; HUD Nevada County FY2026 low-income limit $100,100) + aired Myth One script; fact-checked 2026-06-11 vs ASPE / Federal Register / HUD FY2026 limits.
- **Freshness:** FPL refreshes each January (2026 guideline $33,000/family-of-4, published Jan 15, 2026). HUD county limits refresh each spring (FY2026 effective May 1, 2026). Re-verify both before recording or re-posting after January 2027.

--- SCRIPT ---

A family of four making sixty-six thousand dollars a year
can still qualify for help.
Sixty-six thousand.

People hear "low income" and picture almost nothing.
Here's the truth: the federal poverty line for a family of
four this year is thirty-three thousand dollars -- and many
assistance programs count you as low income at up to DOUBLE
that.

And for housing help it goes even higher. The federal
housing agency -- it's called HUD -- draws its own line,
county by county. Here in Nevada County, their official
low-income line is just over ONE HUNDRED THOUSAND dollars
a year for a family of four.

Every program draws its own line. And almost every line is
higher than people guess. So don't guess -- check.

Dial two-one-one. Go to findhelp dot org and type in your
ZIP code. Or visit techempower dot org and join our
Discord -- we answer.
