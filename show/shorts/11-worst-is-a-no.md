--- SCRIPT ---

Here is the WORST thing that can happen when you apply
for benefits and don't qualify: somebody tells you no.

That's it. That's the whole horror story.

If you apply honestly and it turns out you don't qualify,
there's no penalty. None. Being WRONG is fine -- honest
mistakes are not fraud. Knowingly giving false information
is the one thing that carries penalties -- so just answer
truthfully.

And you stay in control: you can withdraw your application
any time before there's a decision -- no penalty -- and
apply again later whenever you want.

One caution: if anyone in your household isn't a citizen,
get free advice from a legal-aid nonprofit before you
apply.

It's scary to do anything new. But this is the kind of
scary that can end with money in your pocket and food on
your table.
