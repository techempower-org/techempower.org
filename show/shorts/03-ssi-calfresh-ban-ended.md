# On SSI? The Food-Benefits Ban Ended in 2019

- **Est. length:** ~50 seconds (≈135 spoken words)
- **Source:** Ep3 — claim `cf-ssi-seniors` (verdict: accurate). CDSS "CalFresh Food Benefits for SSI/SSP Recipients" page: AB 1811 reversed the cash-out policy effective June 1, 2019; "for decades" holds (cash-out dated to 1974). Verified 2026-07-01.
- **Freshness:** Evergreen (2019 law). CDSS H.R.1 FAQ showed NO 2026 federal change to SSI/CalFresh eligibility as of Jul 1, 2026 — re-scan CDSS if federal food-benefit rules change again.

--- SCRIPT ---

If you're on S-S-I and somebody told you that you can't
get food benefits -- that answer expired in twenty
nineteen.

S-S-I is the monthly federal check for seniors and people
with disabilities living on very little. And for DECADES,
California really did lock S-S-I recipients out of food
benefits. So a lot of folks "know" the answer is no.

That rule is GONE. Since June of twenty nineteen, you can
get both: your S-S-I check AND CalFresh -- California's
grocery money, on a card that works like a debit card at
the store.

If somebody told you no years ago, it's a new answer now.
Go ask again.
