## Why this episode (season-addendum framing)
The season was planned as **five** episodes, and Ep5's close says "every episode this season." **Ep6 is an explicit bonus/encore beyond the five** — the cold open acknowledges that on-camera so the Ep5 line stays true. Two reasons for the encore: (1) the "Getting Connected" (Ep2) topic resonated with viewers who live where cell signal is unreliable; (2) a statewide phone-service story broke (AT&T retiring copper landlines) that makes rural connectivity timely. **Framing is solutions-led and empowerment-toned** — what's happening, why it matters for people like you, how to protect yourself, and (optional) how your voice counts.

## Positioning vs. the standard format
Unlike the usual "2–4 benefits" episode, Ep6 is a **solutions/how-to** episode. It still runs as Shawna-interviews-Jeff dialogue and keeps the standing close/resources. The AT&T copper story is **statewide CONTEXT, not a local emergency** — **Nevada County is NOT among the wire centers AT&T is retiring** (verified against AT&T's filed application), so there is **no local-alarm beat**.

## Segment lineup (beats)
1. **Cold open** — acknowledge the bonus episode; name the theme: keeping a phone that works where the terrain and the power fight you.
2. **The copper story, honestly** — statewide context (AT&T got a federal OK to retire copper for ~184k CA homes; nothing before June 2027; not here). Why copper mattered (self-powers in an outage; AT&T's own filing admits its replacement won't do 911 without power). Pivot to self-help.
3. **Which of the 3 networks works at YOUR house** — maps are guesses; **test free with the library LifeLine hotspot** (callback to Ep2); read real signal (dBm); CellMapper.
4. **Make it better** — the cheap→pricier ladder (directional antenna → hotspot/router → booster), with the honest caveat that a booster amplifies existing signal and can't create one.
5. **Power-user tier (~60–90s)** — a taste (find your tower + its band → band-lock → band-matched antenna) that points viewers to the companion resource page for the full how-to.
6. **Keep it working in an outage** — battery/solar for your hotspot/router; the copper lesson applied to modern gear.
7. **Afford it, honestly** — ACP ended (no replacement); LifeLine covers service, not equipment; the free levers (library, device refurbishers); a "signal-kit lending" community idea.
8. **Close** — recap; optional, non-combative "have your say" (CPUC rulemaking R.24-06-012, Public Advisor 1-866-849-8390); standing resources.
