Jeff - J1Farm money -- FSA microloan
  • Microloan (FSA -- the U-S-D-A's lending arm): up to $50,000 to start or run a small farm, no minimum -- a few thousand for fencing is a perfectly good microloan. Application stripped down for first-timers, small operations, niche -- market gardens, organic growers, beekeepers.
  • NOT needed: land (a lease works) or farm years (apprenticeship / approved mentor counts instead).
  • IS needed: a written business plan + credit you can defend (no minimum score -- do you pay people back) + collateral, which can be what the loan buys (crop, livestock, equipment).
  • Interest "around five percent" -- resets every month, say "check the current rate." Repay up to 7 years.
  • Pays for: seed, feed, livestock, bees, fencing, hoop houses, irrigation, tools -- even organic certification.
  • Buying a farm: sister microloan, same $50,000 cap, higher experience bar -- start with operating. Honesty beat: these exist for folks who can't get a bank loan yet -- the program working as designed.
Jeff - J2EQIP + the federal doors
  • E-QUIP (Environmental Quality Incentives Program -- the U-S-D-A's soil-and-water agency): agree on a conservation project -- irrigation upgrades, livestock fencing, a high tunnel, fire-safety brush clearing.
  • Pays back up to 75% of the cost -- beginning farmers (first 10 years count) can rise to as much as 90%. Both numbers ALWAYS wear "up to."
  • Beginners get at least half up front, before the work starts -- "so you're not floating it."
  • Local proof: more than $4 million into Nevada County land since the nineteen-nineties.
  • Doors: NRCS Grass Valley -- 113 Presley Way, Suite 1 (Valerie Bullard, district conservationist) 530-798-5527. Farm-LOAN office: farmers.gov locator or 2-1-1 + say "farm" -- never name an office.
