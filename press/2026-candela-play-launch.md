# Grass Valley Nonprofit Launches Free App That Reads Help Guides Out Loud — No Internet Required

**GRASS VALLEY, Calif. — [DATE]** — TechEMPOWER, a Nevada County
501(c)(3) nonprofit focused on digital equity, today released
**Candela**, a free Android app that reads written guides and books out
loud with natural neural voices — entirely on the device, with no
internet connection required after setup.

Candela was built for the people TechEMPOWER serves every day: readers
with low vision, adults who struggle with written English, and rural
households where a data connection is a luxury. The app ships with every
TechEMPOWER guide — free cell service, low-cost internet, food benefits,
and more — and reads them aloud while highlighting each sentence, so
listeners can follow along and build reading confidence.

Beyond TechEMPOWER's own guides, Candela reads from more than 20 free
sources, including Project Gutenberg's 70,000+ public-domain books,
LibriVox audiobooks, Wikipedia, RSS feeds, and users' own EPUB files.

"[DRAFT QUOTE — JP TO APPROVE OR REWRITE: 'A lot of our neighbors can't

Candela is free, open-source, and carries no ads, no tracking, and no
analytics — nothing leaves the device without the user's explicit
action. The voice engine runs entirely offline using open neural
text-to-speech models.

## About TechEMPOWER

TechEMPOWER is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in Grass Valley,
California, providing free technology resources to low-income
individuals and families — including step-by-step guides, a searchable
database of 250+ assistance resources, a 2-minute benefits screener at
techempower.org/qualify, and "Wait, I Qualify?!", a benefits-education
video series produced with Nevada County Media. Everything TechEMPOWER
