Hi — this is the first-ever TechEmpower newsletter, so let's start with the
deal: a few times a year, you'll get one short email when we publish
something new or an assistance program changes. No spam, no fundraising
pitches, one-click unsubscribe at the bottom. That's the whole arrangement.

Since it's our first issue, here's the tour of everything we make — all of
it free.

## The website: [techempower.org](https://techempower.org)

Built around one idea: you (or someone you love) probably qualify for help
you're not using. Start with our step-by-step guides — free and low-cost
internet, free cell service, checking your EBT balance, password managers,
and more. Written in plain language, no jargon.

<a href="https://techempower.org"><img src="https://techempower.org/newsletter/issue-01/site-duo.png" width="600" border="0" style="max-width:100%;height:auto" alt="techempower.org on a phone — the home page and the 2-minute benefits screener, in dark mode"></a>

*(Yes, it has a gorgeous dark mode — it follows your phone's setting.)*

## "Wait, do I qualify?" — find out in 2 minutes

Our free screener checks your household against **67 assistance programs**:
food, internet, utilities, health care, and more. No account, no phone
number — your answers never leave your browser. En español también.

→ **[techempower.org/qualify](https://techempower.org/qualify)**

## More than 250 resources, one searchable place

Food distributions, housing help, utility discounts, transportation, legal
aid — organized and searchable at
**[techempower.org/resources](https://techempower.org/resources)**. Focused
on Nevada County, useful well beyond it.

<a href="https://techempower.org/resources"><img src="https://techempower.org/newsletter/issue-01/resources.png" width="420" border="0" style="max-width:100%;height:auto" alt="The resources library on a phone — searchable, with live categories like Car, Children, Farmer, Nonprofit, Senior, Student, and Tech"></a>

**Know something we missed?** If a program or group helped you, tell us at
**[techempower.org/submit](https://techempower.org/submit)** and we'll check
it out and add it for the next person.

## Two free apps we made

<img src="https://techempower.org/newsletter/issue-01/icon-candela.png" width="26" height="26" alt="" style="vertical-align:-6px;border-radius:6px"> **[Candela](https://techempower.org/candela)** — an Android app that reads
every TechEmpower guide out loud, even with no internet. Built for low
vision, limited literacy, or listening while you drive.

<a href="https://techempower.org/candela"><img src="https://techempower.org/newsletter/issue-01/candela.png" width="330" border="0" style="max-width:100%;height:auto" alt="Candela reading the How to use TechEmpower.org guide out loud, highlighting each sentence as it speaks"></a>

<img src="https://techempower.org/newsletter/issue-01/icon-forage.png" width="26" height="26" alt="" style="vertical-align:-6px;border-radius:6px"> **[Forage for All](https://forage.techempower.org)** — a community map of
fruit trees, berries, and edible plants on public land. Drop a pin, share
what's ripe, eat free from your own neighborhood. Web, iOS, and Android.

<a href="https://forage.techempower.org"><img src="https://techempower.org/newsletter/issue-01/forage.png" width="600" border="0" style="max-width:100%;height:auto" alt="Forage for All — a free, open map of food on public land"></a>

Both are open-source: no ads, no tracking.

## From the workshop

Every issue, this corner features one free, open-source thing we've built.
